John Henry's Life:

Many questions still surround this folk lore hero however one truth stands the test of time - John Henry fought the machines and John Henry won. It's said John Henry was born sometime during the 1840's however there is no firm confirmation on a date. What we do know is thanks to Guy B. Johnson, a Professor of Sociology at the University of North Carolina, who in the late 1920's investigated the this famous folk lore tale. From John Henry's Wikipedia page "He concluded that John Henry was a real person who worked on and died at the Chesapeake and Ohio Railway's (C&O Railway) Big Bend Tunnel. The tunnel was built near Talcott, West Virginia, from 1870 to 1872 (according to Johnson's dating)." Thanks to Mr. Johnson we've confirmed John Henry was a real person - woohoo! So why is John Henry famous?

As the 1800's were coming to a close the industrial revolution was full steam ahead. John Henry was known as the hardest worker when it came to railroad labor but when machines started taking his co-workers jobs, John Henry had had enough. The story goes as follows "When the agent for the steam drill company brought the drill here," said Mr. Miller, "John Henry wanted to drive against it. He took a lot of pride in his work and he hated to see a machine take the work of men like him." So the stage was set and the battle begun - as the story goes "At the end of the day-long competition, John Henry had driven more steel with his 14-pound hammer than the mechanical contraption, but he died a martyr on the spot, exhausted by his Herculean efforts or perhaps from heartbreak on realizing what inevitably lay in store."
